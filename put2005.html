Cole's Showcase of Mathematical Endeavours

2005 Putnam Exam Problem B3

Apply the substitution \( x\mapsto{}\tfrac{a}{u} \) and observe that this yields \[ f\big( \tfrac{a}{u} \big)=\tfrac{a}{uf^{\prime}(u)}\] Taking \( u\mapsto{}x \) and taking the derivative with respect to \( x \) on both sides of the equation, we find that \[ \begin{align*} f^{\prime}(\tfrac{a}{x})x^{-2} &= \tfrac{1}{x^2}f^{\prime}(x)^{-1}+\tfrac{1}{x}\ \tfrac{f^{\prime \prime}(x)}{f^{\prime}(x)^2}\\ f^{\prime}(\tfrac{a}{x}) &= \tfrac{1}{f^{\prime}(x)}+x\ \tfrac{f^{\prime \prime}(x)}{f^{\prime}(x)^2} \end{align*} \] Since we have that \( f^{\prime}\big(\tfrac{a}{x}\big)=\tfrac{x}{f(x)} \), we may use this to conclude that \[ \tfrac{1}{f^{\prime}(x)}+x\ \tfrac{f^{\prime \prime}(x)}{f^{\prime}(x)^2} = f^{\prime}(\tfrac{a}{x}) = \tfrac{x}{f(x)}\] We may then equate the left and right hand sides of the previous equation and simplify to see that \[ \tfrac{1}{x}=\tfrac{f^{\prime}(x)}{f(x)}-\tfrac{f^{\prime \prime}(x)}{f^{\prime}(x)}. \] We may then use the identity that \[ \tfrac{f^{(n+1)}(x)}{f^{(n)}(x)}=\frac{d}{dx}\log(f^{(n)}(x)) \] and integrate both sides to see that \[ C_0x=\tfrac{f(x)}{f^{\prime}(x)} \Rightarrow{} \tfrac{f^{\prime}(x)}{f(x)}=\tfrac{\kappa_0}{x}=\frac{d}{dx}\log(f(x))\] Integrating both sides, we conclude -- after some symbolic manipulation -- that \[ f(x)=\kappa_{1}x^{\kappa_0} \] for some unknown constants $\kappa_0,\kappa_1$. We may trivally obtain the fact that $\kappa_0\kappa_{1}^{2}a^{\kappa_0-1}=1$ from calculating $f^{\prime}(a/x)$ in terms of the unknown coefficients and equating the coefficients of $\tfrac{x}{f(x)}$. We may also then observe that $f(1)=\kappa_1=\tfrac{1}{\kappa_0\kappa_{1}a^{\kappa_0-1}}=1$ and thus that $\kappa_1=1$. Plugging this value into our solution limitation yields that $\kappa_{0}a^{\kappa_{0}-1}=1$, which has the special case where $\kappa_0=1$ and holds true for all $a$. Solving this equation, we conclude that \[ f(x)=x^{r}\text{ with }a=r^{\tfrac{1}{1-r}}\text{ if }r\ne{}1\text{ else }a>0. \]
